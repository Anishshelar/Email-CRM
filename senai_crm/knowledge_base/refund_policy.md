# Refund Policy

## Standard Refund Window
Customers are eligible for a full refund within **14 days** of the original payment date.
After 14 days, refunds are not available under the standard policy.
To request a refund, submit a written request to billing@company.com with the invoice number and reason.
Refunds are processed within **5–10 business days** to the original payment method.

## Exceptions Outside the 14-Day Window
Support managers may approve exceptions for:
- Documented platform outages that caused measurable business impact (attach the incident ticket number).
- Billing errors made on our side (duplicate charge, wrong plan applied).
- First-time customers within 30 days of their first payment (goodwill policy, one-time use).

Exception requests must be submitted to billing@company.com with supporting documentation.

## Credits vs. Cash Refunds
For ongoing customers, account credits are the preferred resolution:
- Credits are applied immediately to the account balance.
- Credits never expire and apply to any future invoice.
- Cash refunds require manager approval and take 5–10 business days.

## Churn Retention Playbook
When a customer requests a refund, threatens to cancel, or expresses dissatisfaction:

**Step 1 — Acknowledge immediately (within 1 hour)**
Validate the frustration without being defensive. Do not argue about the 14-day window in the first message. Example: "I'm sorry to hear you've had this experience. Let me look into this right away."

**Step 2 — Identify root cause before making any offer**
- Billing dispute or overcharge → escalate to billing team; resolve within 2 hours.
- Service outage or platform issue → issue a 1-month account credit automatically.
- Feature dissatisfaction or product gap → offer a complimentary 30-minute product walkthrough call.
- Competitive pricing pressure → flag to VP Sales for a custom pricing review.

**Step 3 — Escalation threshold**
If the account value exceeds **$500/month**, escalate to the Customer Success Manager within 1 hour. The CSM owns the retention conversation from that point forward.

**Step 4 — Offer sequence (follow in order)**
1. 1-month account credit for any documented service issue.
2. 2-month account credit for high-value accounts (>$500/month) with persistent or recurring issues.
3. Custom pricing review — requires VP Sales approval.
4. Full refund as a last resort — only if within the 14-day window or if an approved exception applies.

**Step 5 — Document in CRM**
Log the outcome under category "Churn Retention" regardless of whether the customer stayed or left.

## Non-Refundable Items
- Setup fees and professional services engagements are non-refundable.
- Partial months are not refunded on downgrade; credits are issued instead.
- API overage charges are non-refundable once processed.
